I just left this morning's keynote to take an emergency call from my wife (her iPod wouldn't boot). Thankfully, Eric Rudder had already announced Expression, Microsoft's entry in to the design world. The Expression team gave a whirlwind demo of the tools, which include Acrylic, a raster/vector graphics editing tool; Sparkle, a vector animation tool; and Quartz, a webdesign/layout tool. Each application makes heavy use of XAML, uses the MS-Build engine internally,and output integrates seamlessly with Visual Studio 2005.

I'm very interested in the reaction from the design community, as acceptance of these products would significantly streamline the developer-designer workflow.

You can read more about Expression in Microsoft's official press release.
