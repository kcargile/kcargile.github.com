If you've spent much time around developers, you know that we can be pretty hard-headed (me especially). Personally, I've found myself in many heated arguments about why I think (or require, if I can) that its best to not use DataSets in web applications. These debates almost always lead to a complex and passionate white-boarding session, after which everyone leaves unconvinced. Like I said, we're a hard-headed bunch.

Thankfully, ASP.NET guru Scott Mitchell has posted an excellent article on 4GuysFromRolla that tactfully outlines the disadvantages of using a DataSet in an ASP.NET application, complete with metrics and references to some other excellent related resources (e.g. binding custom collections). Maybe Scott's expertise can be used to sway some of the reader-collection naysayers.

Check it out at http://aspnet.4guysfromrolla.com/articles/050405-1.aspx
