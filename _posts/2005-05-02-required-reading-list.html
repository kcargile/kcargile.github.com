Questions I hear frequently from developers are, what are the best practices for developing an API? and, whats the best architecture for my classes? or, how do I correctly design an n-tier application?

I always get excited when people ask questions like this, because they are indicators for three very important qualities in a software developer: software is probably being designed by this person, there is acknowledgment of best practice, and the m.o. is efficacy.

Yet, there's usually no simple answer. Staying ahead of the proverbial curve is a full-time endeavor itself one that requires dedication, focus, and attention to detail. Mastering the fundamentals is critical, and this quality is very often what sets a true engineer apart from the crowd. Remember, its not the length of experience that matters it is the experience itself.

In any event, I do have a suggested reading list that I share with anyone who asks, and I'm always open to new recommendations:

  • Agile Project Management with Scrum
  • Code Complete
  • Design Patterns
  • Microsoft Design Guidelines for Class Library Developers
  • Microsoft Patterns and Practices
  • The New Methodology
  • Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture
  • Refactoring: Improving the Design of Existing Code
  • Test-Driven Development in Microsoft .NET
  • UML Distilled: A Brief Guide to the Standard Object Modeling Language, Third Edition
