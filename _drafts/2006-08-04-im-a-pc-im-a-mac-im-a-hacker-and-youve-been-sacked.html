Whether you fall into the PC camp, the Mac camp, or could care less, you have to admit that Apple's recent "I'm a PC, I'm a Mac" ad campaign is pretty funny. The television spots, which feature the prototypical nerd as "PC" paired with the hipster "Mac", demonstrate what agency mass-marketing does best -- persuade with little regard for the facts.

All operating systems have their faults. It's doubtful that a hacker cares whether you're a PC or a Mac when he's lifting your credit card number or keylogging your ETrade password. And Apple is doing its customers a great disservice by misleading them into believing that they are immune from these types of attacks.

Case-in-point: Jon Ellch and David Maynor have recently demonstrated how OSX can be hijacked in under one minute.

Now might be a good time to patch-up. Use the hard line.
